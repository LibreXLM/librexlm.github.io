The Basics

Stellar is an open-source, decentralized blockchain network designed to connect the world's financial systems. Its primary focus is on fast, low-cost payments and asset issuance. With Stellar, anyone can create, send, and trade digital representations of all forms of money ‚Äì dollars, euros, pesos, or crypto assets.

How it Works

Unlike many other blockchains that rely on energy-intensive mining, Stellar uses a consensus mechanism called the Stellar Consensus Protocol (SCP). This system allows transactions to be confirmed in a few seconds with very low fees (fractions of a cent).

The Stellar network is often described as a kind of global decentralized exchange. Users can issue tokens, create Liquidity Pools, or trade directly on the built-in orderbook.

The Mission

Stellar‚Äôs goal is to make money more accessible, efficient, and inclusive. Billions of people worldwide are unbanked or underbanked. Stellar wants to provide these communities with tools to access digital finance without needing traditional banks.

The non-profit Stellar Development Foundation (SDF) supports this mission. Their focus is on growing the ecosystem, supporting developers, ensuring the stability of the network, and forming partnerships with organizations ranging from NGOs to global payment providers.

Why it Matters

In a world where financial systems are fragmented and often inefficient, Stellar offers a path toward global interoperability. It envisions a future where sending money is as seamless and cheap as sending an email, and where new kinds of assets (like your own personal token) can thrive alongside traditional currencies.
